Accommodation

The venue has dormitory bunk rooms each containing 22 beds and 2 beds in a leaders’ room. Dorms have access to toilet and shower facilities via a covered walkway. Mattresses are provided, but you will need to provide your own linen, blankey and pillow. You are welcome to bring tents if you wish to camp!

Diets & Menu

Tea, Coffee, Beer, Wine, Breakfast, Morning Tea, Lunch, Afternoon Tea, Dinner and Supper will be provided. Please update your ticket details if you have special dietary requirements, we can accommodate both vegan and vegetarian diets.

Coffee

We’re bringing some Caffitaly coffee pod machines, and we’ll be supplying a variety of coffee pods, but feel free to bring your own coffee devices and/or pods!

Code of Conduct

CampJS attendees must read our code of conduct and know we are very serious about making sure CampJS is a safe and welcoming environment for everyone. We will not tolerate any sort of harassment or exclusionary activities. All attendees are expected to use their most mature, inclusive and least-prejudiced behaviour for the duration of the event.

If you’d like to report any issues, do not hesitate to approach Wei Lu or Tim Oxley. Alternatively, email us both at codeofconduct@campjs.com.

THINGS TO BRING

Warm Clothes

e.g. Jackets, Scarves, gloves, onesies. It’s going to be pretty cold.

Swimmers

The venue is right next to the ocean! Please bring appropriate swimwear, especially if you’re planning to participate in the NodeBoats workshop. We have also organised to use the venue’s Kayaks.

Sleeping Bag + Pillow

It’s going to be rather cool weather. I recommend bringing a sleeping bag. Maybe pack an extra blanket. If you cannot bring such items the venue has packs that can be hired for $30.

Earplugs

I cannot guarantee it won’t be noisy at night. If you’re concerned about getting a good night’s rest without pooping anyone’s parties, please bring along earplugs. I personally sleep with earplugs every night and it is bliss for me and convenient for others. If you don’t bring earplugs, I don’t want to hear you complaining about noise.

Bathroom equipment – including Towel

The usual – towel, toothbrush, shampoo, soap, deodorant, etc.

Board/Card Games

Got a cool, modern/ancient board/card game? Bring it along!

Snacks to Share

If everyone brings a little something to share there will always be something to nibble on. Please bring enough snacks to share :D

Computer, Phone and Associated Chargers

Power boards will be available but probably bring more (please put your name on them so we don’t accidentally steal them).

Headphones

We are honoured to have DJ Vagg providing music in the main hall, but if you want to listen to something different, please do so from the comfort of your own headphones.

Instruments

Many of us are musicians, I would love to be able to set up some jam sessions… especially if we can hook it up to some .js! Please bring guitars, mics, tubas, drums, etc.

Medications

Death and illness is likely to be inconvenient for everyone involved, so please don’t forget to bring anything that may prevent incidents.

Warm Clothes

Remember to bring warm clothes.

Tools for coping with no Internet

We will work to have a great intranet but you should not rely on having any Internet. There will be a copy of cdnjs and npm on the intranet.

Here are some essentials you may want to install/familiarise yourself with prior to CampJS V:

  • node/npm: Installation guide by Benjamin Lupton
  • Google Chrome/Canary
  • git
  • Homebrew, if you’re on OSX
  • Dash for OSX, great offline documentation viewer
  • A decent text editor. If you’re not already using vim, then perhaps try SublimeText, Atom or something like WebStorm/WebMatrix
  • Windows users may want to dual boot to linux, or a VM or installing a unix-like terminal such as cygwin

Hardware to play with

If you’ve been playing with or building any cool hardware, bring it along and show it off.

Book Swap

Are you going to read all those books in the next year? No, probably not, be realistic! Please bring along any half-decent technical books you’re not going to read so someone else can!
